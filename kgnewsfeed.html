The Office of the Member for Kiriwina Goodenough supplies medicine and food to the district

09 May 2020

"My office continues to do what it can do to assist my people amid the covid-19 pandemic", said the local MP Hon. D.Tomuriesa as he was responding to critics on social media. He mentioned, apart from his ongoing priorities such as Education, Health, etc.. his office continues to make certain, the wellbeing of Kiriwina Goodenough people are safeguarded during the Government's covid-19 SOE.

Three weeks ago, two chartered flights of medicine were airlifted to the district from Port Moresby. This was done after his announcement on social media as quoted "To my good people of Kiriwina Goodenough Ì want to assure you all we are doing our best to stock all drugs required for our hospitals and by next week we should have everything up to date for the Health Centres down to the Aid Posts. Flights carrying all the medical drugs and other necessities have been dispatched starting today, Friday and next week. We diverted out attention to this urgent need and we are kindly asking for everyone's understanding in this great time of concern."

Food supplies (bags of rice, bags of flour, tinned stuff, etc..) are loaded in four cargo boats and headed to the district to be distributed to the people as part of the KG office's move to assist the district amid this pandemic.

Bolubolu Primary School supported with a funding of K150 000

13th November 2019

After the third 2019 Kiriwina Goodenough District Development Authority (KGDDA) meeting on the 13th of November at Bolubolu Goodenough Island, a special ceremony was conducted. The local MP and the Chairman of the KGDDA board, Hon. D. Tomuriesa presented more than 10 cheques to varies Goodenough Schools, Churches, Cooperatives and comminuties. This was inline with MP's commitment on behalf of his DDA board in supporting its district to move forward.

The Honorable Member briefly mentioned after presenting the K150 000 funding for Bolubolu Primary School about the court battle he fought that took up a lot of his time from serving his people but now that he won the case he is more determine to continue making sure the services for his district are delivered. He stressed that he is the believer of Education. The Scholarship Program that his board initiated had produced more that 1600 students so far and still continuing. He is targeting to double the number in the next couple of years.

The K150 000 presented to the school was the ground-breaking funding to kick start the plans and preparations to get the Bolubolu Primary School earn its Lower High School status in the near future.

2019 Independence Celebrations on Kiriwina Island

16th September 2019

'We are confident and determined as a people to take on our district aspirations on Cultural/Traditional Education for our children and Tourism promotion and development for the district as a government priority', stated the Organising committee Chairman, Dr. Tosiyeru. The chairman added that, bringing communities together promotes harmony and forge ways to addressing many important issues at community level through mutual understanding and respect, including law and order issues. He urged the people to take ownership of the programmes so that Government can come in to support the agendas.

The Chairman also aknowledged the Local Member, Hon Douglas Tomuriesa and his LLG president Hon. Tom Cameron for availing the necessary financial commitment to drive Kiriwina communitys' National Independence Celebrations and Tourism/Cultural Education agenda for now and tomorrow generations.

As a learning curve, Dr. G Tosiyeru reiterated that despite time and resource limitations, he was pleased to say that the leaders embraced a positive enabling attitude and mindset as concerned team of Leaders, and with the help of stakeholders, the district is able to stage this event. Having all the communities present and witnessing the very important occasion is part of the overall success we anticipated to achieve as a Committee, he concluded.

Formal traditional presentation after the Crowning

15th June 2016

Hon. Tomuriesa's recent visit with his delegates to Goodenough LLG was evidently one of the most important visits he and his delegates had ever done over the last four years on the Island. The reception by the people were owesome.

The purpose of the visit was for their Local Hon. Member D. Tomuriesa to officially open various projects his office had funded on the Island like Classrooms, community Halls etc... The member also presented cheques for various groups like Goodnough Cocoa Growers Cooperatives, and some other community groups.

As a token of great appreciation for what the Member had done to the people of Goodnough, the chiefs and people have decided that it was fitting they traditionally initiate him by crowning their member to become the true "SON Of Goodenough". That was the moment the people of Goodenough openly declared they fully support Tomuriesa for what he had done for them for the last 4 years as their local member.
